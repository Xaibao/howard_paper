Water Sample Analysis and FOG Treatment Response
==================================================

Water (Clean/Reference):
- Clean water shows high transmittance across 400-700 nm visible range
- Minimal absorption peaks in the spectral profile
- Used as baseline/reference in the 5-class classification system
- Spectral correlation with palm oil: 0.991 (nearly identical - major challenge)
- Classification accuracy: 100% recall in V2 system
- When water is correctly identified, it means no significant FOG contamination detected

Rapid Assessment Protocol (Using Spectral Monitoring System):
1. Robotic sampling: UAV/USV deploys spectrometer at target water site
2. Spectral acquisition: 300-1000 nm range, 1280-pixel resolution
3. AI classification: MLP-Transformer model identifies substance in <1 second
4. Risk assessment: System provides contamination level and response recommendation

Contamination Level Classification:
- Level 0 (Clean Water): No FOG detected, monitoring continues
- Level 1 (Trace FOG): <10 mg/L, enhanced monitoring, source investigation
- Level 2 (Moderate FOG): 10-100 mg/L, containment recommended, notify authorities
- Level 3 (Severe FOG): >100 mg/L, immediate emergency response required

Emergency Response by Contamination Type:

FOR MOTOR OIL (Level 3 - Immediate Response):
- Deploy containment booms immediately
- Contact environmental emergency hotline (hazardous material)
- Use oil skimmers and absorbent pads
- Document source for legal action
- Long-term monitoring for PAH contamination

FOR PALM OIL / OLIVE OIL (Level 2-3 - Urgent Response):
- Aerate water body to restore dissolved oxygen
- Deploy biological treatment (lipase-producing bacteria)
- Check nearby food processing facilities for discharge violations
- Install or inspect grease traps at upstream food establishments
- BOD monitoring for 2-4 weeks

FOR PORK FAT (Level 2-3 - Urgent Response):
- Similar to food oil response
- Inspect slaughterhouses and meat processing plants
- Check sewer grease traps for overflow
- Anaerobic digestion for collected FOG waste
- Monitor for H2S gas in confined sewer spaces (safety hazard)

Long-Term Treatment Technologies:

1. Biological Treatment:
   - Activated sludge with lipid-adapted microorganisms
   - Biofilm reactors: high retention of lipid-degrading bacteria
   - Key organisms: Pseudomonas putida, Bacillus subtilis, Candida antarctica
   - Lipase enzymes break C-C bonds in triglycerides → fatty acids → CO2 + H2O

2. Physical/Chemical Treatment:
   - Dissolved Air Flotation (DAF): bubbles attach to oil droplets, float to surface
   - Coagulation/flocculation: aluminum sulfate or ferric chloride
   - Activated carbon adsorption for trace motor oil hydrocarbons
   - Membrane filtration (ultrafiltration) for emulsified oils

3. Advanced Oxidation:
   - UV/H2O2 for PAH destruction (motor oil cases)
   - Ozonation for color and odor removal
   - Fenton reaction (Fe2+ + H2O2) for recalcitrant compounds

Monitoring After Treatment:
- Spectral re-measurement every 30 minutes during active treatment
- Lab analysis: BOD, COD, oil & grease content (EPA Method 1664)
- Visual inspection: absence of sheen, normal water color
- Dissolved oxygen measurement: target >5 mg/L for healthy aquatic life
- Follow-up monitoring: 7 days, 30 days, 90 days post-incident

Regulatory Compliance:
- Report to EPA/environmental authority within 24 hours of Level 3 detection
- Maintain monitoring records for 3 years
- Implement Best Management Practices (BMPs) to prevent recurrence
- Third-party verification of cleanup completion
